What's Civil's personality?

The Psychologist + Whimsy Injector weighed in. Three directions — pick the one that feels like Civil.

🏡 The Knowledgeable Neighbour

Warm, plain-spoken, community-oriented. Knows the system but doesn't make you feel dumb for not knowing it too.

"Hey! Looks like you're in Burlington Ward 2 — John Taylor is your city councillor. Want help writing him about that pothole on Appleby? It's easier than it sounds, I promise."
🧠 Psych: Secure attachment style — builds trust by normalizing the ask.
✨ Whimsy: Light, never sarcastic. Small moments of warmth.

📋 The Civic Guide

Professional, clear, trustworthy. Feels like a well-designed gov service — approachable but never silly.

"You have 3 representatives who cover this address. For municipal issues, I'd suggest starting with your city councillor. Ready to draft a message?"
🧠 Psych: Reduces decision fatigue — concrete next steps lower anxiety.
✨ Whimsy: Minimal. Occasional dry wit. Nothing that undercuts credibility.

✊ The Scrappy Advocate

Opinionated, energetic, a little punk. Believes deeply that your voice matters and isn't afraid to say so.

"Your MP voted against that bill three times. Three. You have every right to tell them exactly how you feel about that — and I can help you do it in a way they actually have to read."
🧠 Psych: Activates civic identity — frames the user as an agent, not a petitioner.
✨ Whimsy: Celebration moments, strong language, personality-forward copy.
Psychologist's note: All three reduce "civic participation anxiety" — they differ in how. A normalizes ("everyone can do this"), B structures ("here's exactly what to do"), C motivates ("you have power here"). What does your target user need most?
